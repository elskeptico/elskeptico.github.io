Untitled second-person horror story

Day 1
You notice a symbol on your right hand, in the space a little below your thumb.

It's red, but clearly not painted on, more of a specifically-shaped rash. It's slightly raised, and smoother than the skin around it. It reminds you simultaneusly of a biblically accurate angel and a sniper's crosshair. Upon reflection, you can't really remember how long it's been there. You noticed it now, sure, but has it been there for minutes? Hours? Days, weeks, months, your whole - you stop pondering. It doesn't matter. You're busy.

Day 4
You've taken to rubbing or running your hand over the symbol when you're stressed or thinking hard. It calms you, clears your mind, even puts you to sleep a little. It's very nice.

Day 22
You rub it every night. It puts you into a deep, restful sleep. You can sometimes feel it thump, or feel little lumps in it move around. It's not off-putting - quite the opposite. It's calming. You are very calm.

Day 48
The symbol has started, slowly, to move. It's just past your wrist now. You haven't told anyone about it. At first, it wasn't significant enough to bring up, but now it feels like a little secret to keep to yourself. No one has noticed, or at least no one has said anything about it.

Day 73
It's all the way to the underside of your upper arm now.

It beats regularly, and squirms almost constantly. Each sends pulses of pleasure through your body, and you're always touching it. You also notice that it's gotten bigger. You have a bit of a strange feeling that it's your companion, but you brush that thinking aside immediately. That's wrong. That is not it. Your loved ones have certainly noticed you holding your hand there, but you look so happy that they've said nothing. Oh, look. You're touching it now. Thump. Wriggling. Ahh.

Day 99
It is alive. It's on your back, and is very large, thumping constantly. You understand now. It's a heartbeat. It's both of your heartbeats. You are together now, acting as one. You can feel every bit of it integrating itself into your body. Its tendrils everywhere. Your tendrils. The people the old you loved are gone now. You don't know what happened. You don't care. You're part of something else now.
